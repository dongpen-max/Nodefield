# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nodefield is for solo researchers, product managers, strategists, writers, and independent creators who need to turn scattered material into a coherent line of thought. They use it during focused desktop work to capture evidence, connect ideas, and identify the next action without first configuring a database or joining a collaborative workspace.

## Product Purpose

Nodefield is a local-first visual knowledge workspace. It combines the immediacy of an infinite canvas with typed cards and durable relationships, then lets the user keep, inspect, and export the result as their own file. Success means a user can move from a blank field to a useful connected map in minutes, close the browser, return without data loss, and leave with a portable file rather than an account dependency.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, inventive. The interface should feel focused enough for long research sessions, exact enough to trust with source relationships, and flexible enough that spatial arrangement still feels personal.

## Anti-references

- Do not imitate Excalidraw's hand-drawn identity or tldraw's product chrome.
- Do not look like a neon AI workflow cockpit with glowing nodes, dark navy surfaces, or constant generation controls.
- Do not become a dense all-in-one knowledge suite where navigation competes with the canvas.
- Avoid decorative cards, oversized marketing copy, purple gradients, glass panels, and novelty controls for familiar actions.

## Design Principles

1. The canvas is the workspace. Chrome stays compact and contextual.
2. Structure emerges through direct manipulation; users should not complete setup forms before thinking.
3. The file belongs to the user. Save, recovery, import, and export are visible product behaviors.
4. Progressive disclosure keeps advanced properties out of the way until an object is selected.
5. Familiar controls and predictable feedback matter more than visual novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All commands need visible keyboard focus, icon buttons need accessible labels and tooltips, and state cannot depend on color alone. Respect reduced-motion preferences, preserve usable contrast at every zoom-independent UI surface, keep touch targets at least 40px on mobile, and ensure the core capture/edit/export workflow is keyboard operable.
